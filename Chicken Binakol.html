Ingredients

  • 2 stalks of lemongrass
  • 1 tbsp of canola oil
  • 1 peeled onion, 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp of fish sauce
  • 3-4 pounds of native chicken
  • 1 bunch of spinach, 1 green papaya, 1 cup buko

Instructions

  1. Cut the stem and leaf ends of the lemongrass. Peel a few layers to get to the white part. Cut into 2-inch lengths and pound with the back of a knife to release flavor. Set aside.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, heat the canola oil. Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, and julienned ginger. Cook until softened.
  3. Add the whole native chicken pieces and cook until the color changes and juices run clear.
  4. Add the fish sauce and cook for about 1 minute.
  5. Add the coconut juice and bring to a boil, skimming any scum that may float on top. Add the prepared lemongrass.
  6. Lower the heat, cover, and continue to cook for about 20 to 25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through.
  7. Add the cubed green papaya and cook for about 3 to 5 minutes or until tender.
  8. Add the young coconut meat and cook until heated through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Add the spinach leaves, pushing them down into the broth. Turn off the heat and cover the pot.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your Chicken and Coconut Soup!
